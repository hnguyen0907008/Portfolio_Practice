CONCEPT

In the age of 3D bioprinting, bioreactors and orthotopic transplants the fantasy of science fiction becomes the reality of modern science.

Regeneration and experimental orthotopic transplantation of bioengineered organs. The process of decellularization of human organs and reanimate them back into the natural scaffold of the original organ. It is essentially washing away the building blocks ‘cells’ of the original organs so that new cells can be sown back into the organ matrix. These are called, ‘laboratory organs’.

This process is the brainchild of Harald Ott and his researchers in the area of stem cell and genetic development at Harvard Medical. Ottlab.mgh.harvard.edu
